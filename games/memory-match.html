About Memory Match

Memory Match is a short concentration game where you turn over hidden cards and try to find matching pairs.

The goal is to remember card positions, complete the board efficiently, and use fewer moves on future rounds.

How to Play

  1. Select one hidden card to reveal its symbol.
  2. Select a second hidden card and compare the two symbols.
  3. Matching cards stay revealed and count toward the match total.
  4. Non-matching cards turn back over after a short pause.
  5. Continue until all pairs are found.
  6. Try to complete the board with fewer moves.

Board and Pair Rules

The board has 8 cards and 4 matching pairs. The symbols are Apple, Star, Heart, and Moon, and each symbol appears exactly twice.

A new game creates a fresh shuffled board before play begins. Pressing New Game also starts over with a newly shuffled board.

Understanding Moves and Results

A move is counted after you reveal two valid cards. Selecting one card by itself does not increase the move counter.

The game is complete when all 4 pairs have been matched. Best Moves records the lowest completed move total saved in this browser, so lower numbers are better.

Match and Mismatch Behavior

When two cards match, they stay revealed and can no longer be selected. The match counter increases by 1.

When two cards do not match, the board is temporarily locked, the message shows that it was not a match, and the cards return to hidden after a short delay.

Tips for Fewer Moves

  • Pay attention to both the symbol and where it appears on the board.
  • Use information from previous mismatches instead of choosing randomly.
  • Scan the board in a consistent pattern so positions are easier to remember.
  • Once you know several positions, focus on completing known pairs first.
  • Try working through one area of the board at a time.

Controls and Accessibility

You can play by clicking cards with a mouse or tapping cards on a touch screen. The cards are standard buttons, so browsers may also allow keyboard activation when a card is focused.

The game relies on visual symbols and card positions, so careful visual comparison is part of the challenge.

Saving and Resetting

Memory Match saves only Best Moves in this browser using localStorage. Current moves, matches, and the current board are reset when a new game starts or the page reloads.

New Game resets moves and matches, clears revealed cards, and creates a newly shuffled board. Reset Statistics removes the saved Best Moves record but does not start a new board by itself. Clearing browser storage may also remove the saved record.
