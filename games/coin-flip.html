About Coin Flip

Coin Flip simulates a simple coin flip with two possible outcomes: Heads or Tails.

It is designed for quick casual decisions, short heads-or-tails challenges, and repeated result tracking when a physical coin is not nearby.

How to Flip

  1. Press the Flip Coin button.
  2. Watch the coin display update with H for Heads or T for Tails.
  3. Read the result text below the coin.
  4. Repeat the flip whenever you need another result.
  5. Use Reset Statistics only when you want to clear the saved totals.

Heads and Tails

The two possible outcomes are Heads and Tails. The coin display shows a large H for Heads or T for Tails, and the message below the coin shows the full result text.

The previous result stays visible until the next flip or reset. Repeated Heads or repeated Tails can happen in normal play.

Understanding the Results

Total Flips is the number of flips saved in this browser. Heads Count and Tails Count show how many times each outcome has appeared.

After a valid flip, Total Flips increases by 1 and either Heads Count or Tails Count increases by 1. The page does not track percentages, streaks, best results, or a flip-by-flip history list.

Random Result Behavior

Each press of Flip Coin asks the game's built-in random-number logic for a new Heads or Tails result.

Either outcome may appear repeatedly, and previous flips do not guarantee what the next result will be.

Common Uses

  • Choose between two casual options.
  • Decide who takes the first turn in a friendly game.
  • Run a simple heads-or-tails challenge.
  • Observe how heads and tails totals change over repeated flips.
  • Replace a physical coin when one is not available.

Controls and Devices

You can flip with a mouse click or by tapping the Flip Coin button on a touch screen. The flip and reset controls are standard buttons, so browsers may also allow keyboard activation when a button is focused.

The flip button is not disabled during the coin animation; pressing it again simply starts another flip result.

Saving, History, and Resetting

Coin Flip saves Total Flips, Heads Count, and Tails Count in this browser using localStorage. These totals can remain after a page refresh.

Reset Statistics removes the saved totals, clears the coin letter, removes the flip animation class, and shows the Statistics reset message. Clearing browser storage may also remove saved coin flip data.
