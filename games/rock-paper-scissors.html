About Rock Paper Scissors

Rock Paper Scissors is a quick classic game where you choose Rock, Paper, or Scissors and the computer chooses one of the same three options.

Each round ends as a Win, Lose, or Draw based on the standard matchup rules. It is built for fast, repeatable casual rounds.

How to Play

  1. Choose Rock, Paper, or Scissors using the three choice buttons.
  2. The computer immediately selects one of the same three choices.
  3. Review the round result: Win, Lose, or Draw.
  4. Read the choice message to see both selections.
  5. Keep playing additional rounds whenever you want.
  6. Use Reset Statistics only when you want to clear the saved totals.

Matchup Rules

Rock defeats Scissors, Scissors defeats Paper, and Paper defeats Rock.

If you and the computer choose the same option, the round is a Draw.

Computer Choice

The computer selects Rock, Paper, or Scissors using the game's built-in random-number logic.

Any valid choice may appear repeatedly, and previous rounds do not determine the next computer choice.

Understanding the Results

The round message shows Win, Lose, or Draw after each valid choice. A second message shows your choice and the computer's choice.

Wins, Losses, and Draws count the saved totals for completed rounds in this browser. The page does not track total rounds separately, streaks, best streaks, score, or match history.

Playing Tips

  • Learn the three matchup rules before trying to play quickly.
  • Remember that repeated computer choices are possible.
  • Do not assume the next computer choice must differ from the previous round.
  • Use several rounds when comparing a session instead of relying on one result.
  • Treat the game as casual play rather than a prediction test.

Controls and Devices

You can play with a mouse click or by tapping Rock, Paper, or Scissors on a touch screen. The choice and reset controls are standard buttons, so browsers may also allow keyboard activation when a button is focused.

The choice buttons are not disabled during the result animation; each click plays a new round immediately.

Saving, History, and Resetting

Rock Paper Scissors saves Wins, Losses, and Draws in this browser using localStorage. These totals can remain after a page refresh.

Reset Statistics removes the saved totals, returns Wins, Losses, and Draws to 0, changes the round message to Statistics reset., and clears the choice message. Clearing browser storage may also remove saved records.
